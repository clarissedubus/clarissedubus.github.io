Botte-Cul Stool

The botte-cul is the name given to the monopode stool used by farmers to milk cows in the valleys of the Alps. It was often attached around the waist using a strap or belt that allowed for ease of movement in the stable. The specificity of purpose of the botte-cul contrasts with the universality of many everyday objects. We often sit and recline without thinking or taking notice of how our bodies are interacting with the environment. The design of the Botte-Cul Stool builds on the historic use of the botte-cul to provide context to an object that enables some of the most repetitive and automatic of our actions. White shoes, oak, metal.
